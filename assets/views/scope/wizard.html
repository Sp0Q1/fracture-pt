Testing Approaches Explained

Crystal-box & Grey-box

Crystal-box gives the tester full access to source code, architecture diagrams, and credentials — the most thorough and efficient approach. Grey-box provides partial information (user-level credentials, limited docs), balancing realism with coverage.

Black-box & Red-team

Black-box simulates an external attacker with zero knowledge — realistic but takes longer. Red-team is a full adversary simulation combining social engineering, phishing, and technical exploitation across multiple vectors.

Scope Wizard FAQ

How accurate is this price estimate?

The estimate is a starting point based on typical engagements. Final pricing depends on target complexity (e.g. a legacy monolith vs. a modern API), specific compliance requirements, and any custom deliverables. Our team will review your configuration and provide a precise quote.

What counts as a "target"?

A target is a distinct testable unit: one web application, one API, one IP range/VLAN, one mobile app, or one code repository. A single server running multiple services may count as multiple targets depending on scope.

Can I change the scope after requesting a quote?

Absolutely. The wizard gives you a starting configuration. During the scoping call, we refine targets, testing windows, and exclusions together. Nothing is final until you accept the formal offer in your dashboard.

What is the difference between man-days and calendar days?

Man-days represent the total testing effort. A 10 man-day engagement could be completed by 2 testers in 5 calendar days, or 1 tester in 10 calendar days. We agree on the schedule during scoping.

Do I need an account to request a quote?

No. The form above works without an account. If you already have an account, you can submit a detailed scope request directly from your dashboard at /engagements/new.
